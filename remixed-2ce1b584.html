Remote Patient Monitoring

Real-time vitals from connected medical devices, analysed by an anomaly-detection model — not invented by AI.

🧠 How does AI actually get heart rate & BP? (no hardware = no data)
1
Physical sensor on the patient — a pulse oximeter, ECG leads, an automatic BP cuff, or a wearable measures the real physiological signal.
2
Device firmware computes HR / SpO₂ / BP locally and streams it over Bluetooth LE or Wi-Fi to a bedside gateway — this is the only source of the numbers, AI does not generate them.
3
Express.js API receives the reading, validates it, and writes it to MongoDB as a time-series document tied to the patient record.
4
Scikit-learn model (trained offline on historical vitals with Pandas/NumPy) reads the stored sequence and flags abnormal trends — it analyses existing sensor data, it cannot "detect" a heartbeat out of thin air.
5
The dashboard below just renders whatever is in the database in real time. Switch patients and you'll need to re-pair a device each time — that's intentional, to keep the hardware dependency honest.
